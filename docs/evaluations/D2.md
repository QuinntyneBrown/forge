# D2 — Evaluate HTML mocks

## Pass 1 - findings

Inspected all 33 PNG screenshots (11 mocks × 3 breakpoints) under `./docs/mocks/screenshots/`. Mechanical checks: all PNGs present, none stale (criterion 1); no `fetch(`, `XMLHttpRequest`, `localStorage`, `sessionStorage`, or relative `.css` links anywhere (criteria 6, 10); class naming is consistent BEM (`block`, `block__element`, `block--modifier`) — `card`, `topbar__title`, `card--elevated`, `btn btn--filled`, etc. (criterion 7); only CDN links (Google Fonts, Material Symbols) appear in `<head>` and there are no `<script>` tags (criterion 10).

Material 3 adherence reads correctly across screens (color roles, type scale, elevation tiers, shape, M3 component anatomy — top app bar, bottom nav / nav rail, FAB, cards, chips, list items, switches, outlined text fields). Coverage matches the idea: equipment-typed sessions, calorie ring with 1500 target, streak gamification, eating-window card, points/rewards economy, Apple Watch integration in profile and error state. Mobile-first layouts at 360 reflow to 2-column at 768 and a navigation rail + grid at 1440.

The following blocking findings were found:

### Finding 1 — Apple/Google OAuth UI in sign-in is out of scope (criterion 8: Coverage)

`./docs/mocks/sign-in.html` lines 99-102 render an "or continue with" divider plus Apple and Google social-sign-in buttons. The workflow's "Authentication & User Management" guidance explicitly states: *"PKCE and external identity providers are explicitly out of scope for this workflow."* Mocking those flows here creates a UI affordance the implementation will never deliver and would mislead R1 / R2 (requirements). The visual hierarchy of the sign-in screen also implies parity between username/password and OAuth that is not real.

Affected screenshots: `sign-in.360.png`, `sign-in.768.png`, `sign-in.1440.png`.

**Fix:** delete the divider and `.social` block from `sign-in.html`; close the auth card after the primary "Sign in" button.

### Finding 2 — workout-detail Notes textarea clips its content (criteria 9, 4)

At 360 the Notes textarea has a fixed visible height that cuts the placeholder/example content below the input border (visible text ends mid-line: *"Saving the harder effort for ... block at 5:48"*). Looks broken even though the field itself is functional. Affected: `workout-detail.360.png`.

**Fix:** either raise `min-height` on the textarea so the multi-line example fits, or shorten the static example to 1-2 lines.

### Finding 3 — profile Time zone select truncated at 360 (criterion 9)

The Time zone field renders `America/Toron` instead of the full `America/Toronto` because the IANA zone string overflows the half-width select at 360. Affected: `profile.360.png`.

**Fix:** make the Time zone select full-width on the smallest breakpoint (single column at 360, then two-column at 768+), and/or use the abbreviated form `ET — Toronto`.

### Finding 4 — sign-in 1440 hero copy sits at bottom of left panel (criterion 4: UX)

On the desktop split layout the "Welcome back." headline plus brand mark cluster near the bottom of the green hero panel rather than near the top. Reading order is awkward and the brand feels demoted. Affected: `sign-in.1440.png`.

**Fix:** anchor the hero content to the top of the panel (e.g., `justify-content:flex-start` with generous `padding-top`) so the eye lands on the headline first.

### Finding 5 — error-state has a large empty gap above the main content at 360 (criterion 4: UX)

After the inline "Apple Watch sync paused" banner, ~600px of empty space appear before the centered watch illustration. The viewport feels broken/loading rather than intentional. Affected: `error-state.360.png` (less pronounced at 768/1440 because the page widens).

**Fix:** reduce the top margin / min-height that pushes the illustration down. The banner + main content should read as a single composed page.

### Non-blocking observations

- Auth screens have generous trailing whitespace because they fit a small form into a `fullPage:true` capture at 2200px height. Side-effect of the renderer, not a real layout failure — leaving as-is.
- `workouts` filter chip strip clips the "Bench" / "Elliptical" / "This week" chips at the right edge at 360. Acceptable because the strip is `overflow-x:auto`; chips scroll horizontally on a real device.
